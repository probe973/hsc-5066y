## Getting Descriptive Statistics
From the data set you are going to find the following descriptive statistics for the age of participants:

- Mean
- Median
- Standard Deviation
- Minimum Age
- Maximum Age
- Range
- 25h Percentile
- 75th Percentile
- Inter-quartile Range

</div>

<div class="instructions" markdown="1">
  
* From the top menu click **Analyze**, **Descriptive Statistics**, and **Explore**
* Place the `Age`, labelled `Chronological age in years`, variable into the **Dependent List** box
* Click the **Statistics** button and choose **Descriptives** and **Percentiles**
* Click **Continue**
* Click **OK**

This should give you some of the descriptive statistics regarding the age of the participants that we are looking for.

</div>

<div class="output" markdown="1">

You should find the following statistics about the age of the participants in the sample.  There will be a **Descriptives** output and a **Percentiles**.

| Statistic  | Age        |
|------------|------------|
| Mean       | 67.2       |
| Median     | 68         |
| S.D.       | 8.42       |
| Min        | 45         |
| Max        | 86         |
| Range      | 41         |
| 25th percentile  | 62       |
| 75th percentile  | 72         |
| IQR        | 10       |

</div>
